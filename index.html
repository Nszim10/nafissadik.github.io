Engineer. Researcher.
Lifelong Learner.

I am a Mechanical Engineering graduate from BUET and currently a Lecturer at the Department of Mechanical Engineering, SUST. My work is primarily focused on simulation and computational analysis — particularly in molecular dynamics and heat transfer.

I am interested in understanding how systems behave at different scales, from nanoscale materials to practical engineering applications. During my undergraduate studies, I worked on thermal conductivity analysis of silicon nanowires and later became involved in research related to solar thermal systems.

Alongside teaching, I continue to learn and sharpen my research skills. I am particularly drawn to advanced simulation tools and plan to pursue a PhD in the future.

I prefer to keep things simple: learn deeply, work honestly, and improve step by step.

02 — Research & Projects

Work That
Drives Me

Undergraduate Thesis
Thermal Conductivity of Silicon Nanowires — Effect of Void Shapes
Comparative analysis of how different void geometries affect the thermal conductivity of silicon nanowires using molecular dynamics simulation. Conducted under Prof. Dr. A. K. M. Monjur Morshed at BUET.
LAMMPS Molecular Dynamics Nanoscale Heat Transfer
Research
Solar Thermal Collector with PID Controllers
Contributed to a study on performance enhancement of a vented quarter-circular solar thermal collector using proportional, PI, and PID controllers. Published in Applied Thermal Engineering (2025).
Solar Thermal PID Control CFD Energy
Undergraduate Project
Food Delivery Drone
Designed and developed an autonomous drone system capable of GPS-based navigation for efficient food delivery applications.
Autonomous Systems GPS Navigation Embedded
Undergraduate Project
Twisted Double Pipe Heat Exchanger
Designed, constructed, and tested a double-tube heat exchanger with twisted fins. Simulated and validated in SOLIDWORKS and HTRI for thermal performance evaluation.
SOLIDWORKS HTRI Heat Exchanger Thermal Analysis
